Print Swap is a platform where photographers and visual artists exchange physical prints directly with each other. No buying, no selling, no metrics. Two people who want to live with each other's work.

Launch exhibition, limited to 50

7 May 2026 The Print Space, Shoreditch

To mark the launch of Print Swap, we're exhibiting 50 prints at The Print Space in Shoreditch. Work is curated from uploads received before 1 May.

Exhibition prints are produced by The Print Space. It is free to enter, and there is no charge if you are selected to be exhibited.

If your work is selected, it hangs on the wall that night. You become a Founding Artist: a permanent identifier in the app, limited to the 50 artists chosen for the opening. Your work goes live for swaps from the moment the platform opens.

The exhibition deadline is 1 May. Print Swap continues after that, but there will only ever be 50 Founding Artists.

Most of your work will never leave a screen.

You've made images worth printing. Work that deserves to be framed, hung, and looked at every day. But the infrastructure around photography is built for selling, exhibiting, or feeding an algorithm. Most photographers don't want any of that.

Print Swap is built around something simpler: finding another photographer whose work you'd clear a wall for, and discovering they feel the same about yours.

No transaction. No gallery cut. No follower count. Two artists who choose each other. The platform runs continuously, so whenever you're ready, the collection is there.

Four steps. Two artists. One exchange.

  1. Upload your work. Choose the image you want to offer. Add your print size and paper preference.
  2. Browse the collection. Find work from photographers across the platform that you'd genuinely want on your wall.
  3. Propose a swap. Send a proposal to that artist. They see your work. They decide.
  4. The exchange begins. If it's mutual, both prints go into production. Each artist covers the cost of their own outgoing print. The Print Space handles production and fulfilment.

Built by Photographers for Photographers.

Print Swap was founded by photographers Sam Faulkner and Simon Roberts. They sell their work, but they are also happy to exchange it with other photographers. They wanted a dedicated space to do that on mutual terms, without pitches or chasing metrics. It didn't exist, so they built it.

You didn't make it to be scrolled past.

Some images aren't meant to stay on a screen. They're meant to be printed, framed, and lived with by someone who looked at your work and thought: I want that on my wall.

That person is already here.
